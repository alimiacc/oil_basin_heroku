Impact of COVID-19 on US Oil Production, Consumption, and Price.

Using the interactive charts below to explore the dataset

By: Andrew Bartczak, Derek Teed,
Nisha Sapkota, and Alimi Mukaila

The outbreak of COVID-19 in the US has caused a dramatic shift in the Oil Industry. The US lockdown and travel restrictions created the largest and quickest drop in demand for oil products in history. Various onshore oil storage facilities had reached their oil storage limits which helped send oil prices negative for the first time ever. The loss in oil demand that caused low oil prices has also caused the quickest and largest drop in US Onshore Oil Rig Count in modern history, which is having a profound effect on the US economy.
