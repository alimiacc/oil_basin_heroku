Predicting the Impact of COVID-19 on US Natural Gas Prices.

Using the interactive charts below to explore the dataset

By: Derek Teed, Nisha Sapkota, and Alimi Mukaila

The outbreak of COVID-19 in the US has caused a dramatic shift in the Natural Gas Industry. The US lockdown and recession in the US is slightly lowering the demand for natural nas. However, the production of natural gas is expected to fall drastically in the next year or two. The loss of US natural gas production is being caused by a large drop in the number of natural gas rigs and oil rigs. The loss of natural gas and oil rigs was cause by a huge drop in the prices of natural gas liquids, condensates, dry natural gas, and oil. The subsequent fall in production is predicted to have a large increase in the price of natural gas as supply falls below demand and storage gets depleted. This should cause a short term rise in the cost of electricity and a rise in natural gas rigs.
